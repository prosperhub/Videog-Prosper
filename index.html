Editing tip WHile changing back-ground

Arrange your media so that the foreground footage is above the background Make sure you select the foreground clip. Then, in the Effects panel, search for Crop and double-click to apply the effect to your footage. In the Effect Controls panel, adjust the Left and Right values to crop out any unused area of the background. Go back to the Effects panel, search for Ultra Key, and double-click to add the effect to the footage. Click the color swatch next to the eyedropper tool in the Effect Controls panel and then select the color you are trying to remove Change the Output to Alpha Channel to get a better look at the remaining shades of the background that need to be removed. Next, adjust the individual settings to make sure the background is clean. We started with setting the Pedestal to 100 and Mid Point to 25 and then fine-tuned the other values to completely remove the background. Press the Tab key to move quickly from one value to the next. When you’re done, change the Output back to Composite.
